Privacy Policy

When you visit our site

Like everyone and their grandmonster, we use Google Analytics to track general site usage. The data we collect is anonymous and consists of an IP address, type of operating system (hopefully OSX!), type of browser (hopefully Chrome!), and how long you stayed (hopefully long enough to adopt a monster!) all of which may be used one day to improve our site (give the people what they want). To be honest, we aren't even smart enough to do anything sneaky with your data and we don't sell it.

Google has some policies that we must mention to use their service. Google Analytics uses first-party cookies to track visitors interactions. Cookies are used by millions of sites and contain information that is transferred to your computer's hard drive and is used to store information such as the time of visit, whether the visitor has been to the site before, and what site referred the visitor to the web page. Browsers do not share first-party cookies across domains.

Google collects all of this information anonymously. Personal information is information that identifies you, such as your name, email address, or billing information, or other data which can be reasonably linked to such information. Google Analytics prohibits the tracking or collecting of this information or associating personal information with web analytics information.

You can opt out of Google's use of cookies without affecting how you visit our site here. Alternatively, you can prevent Google Analytics data from being collected by downloading the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on here.

When you adopt a monster

A marker will be placed on the "Recently Adopted" map in the general area of your city. There is no specific max-min range, it's a bit less complicated. You live at 123 Monster Way, Monster City, Monster State. We'll drop the city name into a geocoder which spits out a long/lat for the middle of that city and then we randomize the trailing numbers a bit to avoid overlaps.

We hope you'd like to receive updates about monster happenings. However, our Twitter will likely be a better and more reliable experience than annoying newsletters. That said, we'll notify you if we plan on starting up something more substantial with email addresses, in which case you'll have an opportunity to opt-out.

Origins

Little is known about where these monsters come from. In fact, even if we did know their specific origins, we wouldn’t disclose it. Secrecy is the best policy, especially in these cautious times.

What we can tell you is this. We’ve stumbled upon a hefty batch of eggs. From what our team has gathered, these monsters hatch in broods – 100 eggs in each. We have no way of knowing what they’ll look like when they hatch, but we can determine their color. It’s all based on exposure to light and temperature, rather straightforward actually. If we want to make a monster blue, we simply place the egg in a dark enclosure. Level of temperature dictates how vibrant a color is. For a bright iridescent blue, we just crank up the heat.

Unfortunately, we have no control of any other feature of the monster. But this is the fun part...You never know what you’re going to get!

100 The First Brood

After much careful and calculated testing, we’re finally ready to debut our first brood of 100 monsters for public adoption. All you have to do is choose a color for your new monster. Now, remember this isn’t the most thorough of science - we’re rather new to this field. But we’ve had rather promising results. We just ask that you don’t be too disappointed if your colors aren’t as imagined. Hopefully we can perfect our colorization process in the future.

We’re always eager for feedback. Or even just an update on how your newly adopted monster is doing. Tweet us @3DollarMonsters, we’d love to hear from you!
